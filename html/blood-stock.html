Blood Product Explanations

  • Whole Blood: Whole blood is the blood collected directly from a donor. It contains red cells, white cells, platelets, and plasma. It is used in trauma cases and surgeries where patients need all blood components.
  • Plasma: Plasma is the liquid part of blood that carries cells and proteins throughout the body. It is used to treat patients with clotting disorders, burns, and shock, and is essential for producing medicines.
  • Platelets: Platelets are small cell fragments that help blood clot. Platelet transfusions are vital for cancer patients, those undergoing chemotherapy, and people with bleeding disorders.
